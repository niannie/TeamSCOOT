Timeline

March 2022

Team Formation


After passing through the extensive Gemstone team selection process, consisting of two rounds of voting by the 2025 Gemstone cohort, Team SCOOT was established by 10 undergraduate students. The attached video created by Jake Muller and Zuzanna Szylow was used to gain interest in the topic and ultimately recruited the most efficient team to carry out the research.

May 2022

Team Mentor


Our team mentor, Dr. Derek A. Paley, was appointed following consultation with Gemstone staff. Paley is the Director of the Maryland Robotics Center and Willis H. Young Jr. Professor of Aerospace Engineering Education in the Department of Aerospace Engineering and the Institute for Systems Research at the University of Maryland. Paley has played a crucial role in our research process by providing insightful feedback, valuable resources, and helpful advice.

August 2022

I-Corps Process and Value Proposition


In the summer of 2022, a few of our members participated in the I-Corps course which gave us the skills to conduct valuable stakeholder interviews in order to refine our value proposition. The I-Corps Customer Discovery process includes interviewing potential customers to understand their problems within the market which allowed our team to pinpoint where the gap in research lies. Ultimately, our team collectively has conducted 92 interviews and as a result, our refined value proposition is to design and prototype an ergonomic and modular knee scooter with a novel kick-assist technology to improve ride ability for more users.

March 2023

Proposal Defense


After spending several weeks with interviews and the I-Corps process, our team presented our intended research direction to our mentor, an expert in the field, as well as the Gemstone staff during our proposal defense. During this event, our team explained the feasibility of our project, timelines, and our projected end goal.

September 2023

Lab Space


Within our third year in the program, our team acquired a lab space in the robotics lab at the E.A. Fernandez IDEA Factory under supervision of our lab manager Ivan Penskiy. SCOOT proceeded to purchase devices and electronics that were relevant to our research goals. This included a SuperHandy Mobility Scooter, a traditional knee scooter, and an ODrive Controller. Special thanks to SCOOT's mentor, Dr. Derek A. Paley for financial contribution towards these resources.

November 2023

Do Good Showcase


Another big milestone of SCOOT was our invitation to present at the Do Good Showcase where we were able to present the value of our research and how our work positively contributes to society. Walking away with the Audience Choice Award, our team hopes to use this encouragement to continue expanding our research horizons, and create a working proof of concept and prototype of our design within the next year.
